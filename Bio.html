Biography

I am a graduate Electrical and computer Engineer, majoring in Machine Learning and Signal Processing. I am currently working at Perception team of Huawei Noah's Ark Research Lab as an Autonomous Vehicle, Machine Learning Research Engineer, where I work on deep learning modules for self-driving vehicles. Previously, I was a Machine Learning Research Engineer at Computer Vision/Model Compression (ANT) team of Huawei Noah's Ark Research Lab, where I worked on quantization techniques of model compression for DNN.

I completed my Masters degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering department at McGill University. I worked under the supervision of Professor Benoit Champagne as a part of the Telecommunication and Signal Processing research team. The focus of my thesis research was on learning based single channel approach for speech enhancement. More specifically, I worked on supervised deep neural network for this problem and published a conference paper at IEEE, ISSPIT in Bilbao, Spain on December 2017. I was also an active member of McGill IEEE student branch committee as a Webmaster, where I designed and maintened IEEE student branch webpage.

I have previous research experience during my final year engineering design project at Ryerson University, where I completed my B.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering department, majoring in Multimedia and digital signal processing. I worked under the supervision of Professor Xavier Fernando on a robotic project with a team member. We built Collaborative Robots for Rescuing Underground Miners and published a paper and won the second prize of IEEE Humanitarian Initiatives Conference (HIC) at Canadian Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (CCECE) competition at Regina on May 2013.
