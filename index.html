Ryan Razani

I am a graduate Electrical and computer Engineer from McGill University, majoring in Machine Learning and Signal Processing. I am currently working at Huawei Noah's Ark Research Lab as an Autonomous Vehicle/Machine Learning Research Engineer, where I work on deep learning modules for self-driving vehicles. I am focused on developement and fundamental research within 2D and 3D semantic segmentation, object detection for deep neural networks.

Previously, I was a Machine Learning Research Engineer at Computer Vision/Model Compression (ANT) team of Huawei Noah's Ark Research Lab, where I worked on model compression for deep neural network. I focused on development of a quantization technique for DNN, aiming to solve the storage and computational limitations of edge devices.

Research and Publications

My research interests lie in the fields of Deep Learning, Computer Vision, and Autonomous Systems. Currently, I am focused on learning-based LiDAR/multi-modal perception of self-driving vehicle. However, I also have strong interests in machine learning, natural language processing, digital signal processing, speech enhancement and recognition, and source seperation.

I completed my Masters degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering department at McGill University. I worked under the supervision of Professor Benoit Champagne as a part of the Telecommunication and Signal Processing research team. The focus of my thesis research was on learning based single channel approach for speech enhancement. More specifically, I worked on supervised deep neural network for this problem and published a conference paper at IEEE, ISSPIT in Bilbao, Spain on December 2017.

I have previous research experience during my final year engineering design project at Ryerson University, where I completed my B.Eng. in Electrical and Computer Engineering department, majoring in Multimedia and digital signal processing. I worked under the supervision of Professor Xavier Fernando on a robotic project with a team member. We built Collaborative Robots for Rescuing Underground Miners and published a paper and won the second prize of IEEE Humanitarian Initiatives Conference (HIC) at Canadian Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (CCECE) competition at Regina on May 2013.

A reduced complexity MFCC-based deep neural network approach for speech enhancement
Ryan Razani, Hanwook Chung, Yazid Attabi, Benoit Champagne
IEEE, ISSPIT in Bilbao, Spain, Dec 2017.

We propose a low-complexity deep neural network for single-channel speech enhancement, by leveraging a spectral feature mapping from noisy mel frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCC) to enhanced short- time Fourier transform (STFT) spectrum.
Collaborative Robots for Rescuing Underground Miners
Ryan Razani, Hooman Samani, and Xavier Fernando
IEEE Humanitarian Initiatives Conference (HIC) at Canadian Conference on Electrical and Computer Engineering (CCECE), Regina , May 2013.

We built collaborative robots for rescuing underground miners. The goal of this research project was for robots to search a predefined square shape area to detect an object and carry that to a predefined coordinate by help of the other robot, while communicating with one another not to crash into each other or the predefined boudries/walls.
